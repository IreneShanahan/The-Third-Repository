That wristband takes a continuous glucose and heart rate reading and updates the app.

Who is the Food Monitor For?

The food monitor is for people who wish to keep a diary of the foods that they are eating. This is for diabetics who need to track their food intake so that they can calculate how much insulin to take. For people who are trying to lose weight, gain weight or maintain their weight. For people who are trying to figure out which foods to eliminate from their diet due to food intolerances.

How do you use the Food Monitor?

The user wears a wristband that is synced up with an app on their smart phone. They log in everything that they eat and drink. The app monitors both the users heart rate and glucose levels. All this information is displayed in text and graphs form. The diary and graphs can be printed out or emailed to their medical professional or fitness instructor.

The Food Diary

A food diary is a record of foods that you eat on a meal-by-meal, daily, or weekly basis. You can include nutritional or other dietary information, organize and track foods by food group. This lets you monitor know what, when, and how often you eat, as well as where your nutrition needs lie.

The heart rate monitor

The wristband monitors the heart rate continuously sending data to the app on your smart phone. A heart rate monitor has become a feature that separates serious fitness trackers from basic step counters. Your heart rate is a telling metric, providing insight into how hard your heart has to work both while resting and during exercise. For serious athletes and people who want to train using target heart rate zones, a heart rate monitor's accuracy makes a huge difference.

Glucose levels

Glucose is a simple sugar, an important energy source in living organisms and is a component of many carbohydrates. People with diabetes need to check their glucose levels every day to maintain good health.
